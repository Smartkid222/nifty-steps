RULES GOVERNING THE CONDUCTS OF SUDENTS AND PARENTS

1. School is a learning institution. We therefore demand of parents that they encourage their children to make all efforts to be at school early on a every school day. Teachers and parents must join hands to teach students to avoid being tardy.
2. Unless attending special after-school lessons at the school, or are compelled to remain at school beyond the hours of closing, all students must leave the school premises immediately the school has closed for the day.
3. For security and safety reasons, parents are advised not to entrust commercial bike (Okada) riders with the picking of their children to and from school.
4. No student is allowed to leave the school premises at any time during school hours without valid exit cards signed by the principal or vice-principal.
5. No student is allowed to use a cell phone in the classroom. In fact, we discourage the practice of students bringing cell phones to school as the use of phones at school can pose unwanted multitasking challenges for children.
6. Students must show respect to other students and to their teachers at all times. Students who constantly exhibit obtrusive and disruptive behaviours may be suspended or asked to withdraw from the school.
7. Parents who wish to lodge a complaint concerning their children must first call the school to arrange for audience with the building principal.. Collaboration

Our Value Statements


The value statements listed below, stem from our core values. They form the standard against which we measure every action, every relationship and every piece of work we engage in as players in the Nifty Steps School environment.
-> We are thorough in everything we do.
-> We respect the rights and personality of the child, the teacher and the parent.
-> We are tolerant of the beliefs and the views of others, irrespective of age, gender, race, or nationality.
-> We co-operate with one another to achieve the best.
-> We are transparent in our relationships, and in all our transactions.

EXTRA CURRICULA ACTIVITIES

A. Students Government


There is a students consultative council established as a forum to train the students on issues of good citizenship, democratic values and consultative principles. The head boy or head girl chairs the meeting of the council which is largely made up of the appointed and elected representatives from all the classes in the college.

B. Clubs and Societies


The school subscribes to the following clubs to which any student can belong. Students are however advised to restrict membership to not more than three clubs in a year in order to avoid activities overload.
-> Girls Guide
-> Literary and debating society
-> Mathematics club
-> Music club
-> Quiz club
-> Spelling Bees
-> The press club

C. Sports and Cultural Activities


Students are grouped into sports houses and various cultural groups at the beginning of every school year, and are made at certain times of the year to showcase their skills in sports, as well as in diverse activities of the cultures that their individual groups represent.
